Integration


Seamless Integration Management is critical for ensuring efficient airport operations by enabling real-time data exchange, system interoperability, and coordinated decision-making.


Key System Integrations & Message Exchanges


Here are some critical airport system integrations and the types of messages exchanged:


  • AODB-ATC:

    Arrival Flight: • Flight Plan filed from outstation, •Take-off from outstation, • 30 miles out, • Twelve minutes out, • Outer marker, • Touchdown, • On Blocks

    Departure Flight: • Flight Plan filed, • Issuance of TOBT, • Start of boarding, • Aircraft ready, • Start up approval time, • off blocks, • Take off

    Stand Allocation: Publish stand allocations from AMS to ATC


  • AODB-BHS:

    Departure Flight: •Flight schedule and flight data updates (AODB to BHS), • Makeup belt allocation (BHS to AODB)

    Arrival Flight: • Carousel allocation and arrival time (AODB to BHS), • First Bag/Last Bag time (BHS to AODB)


  • AODB-BRS:

    Departure Flight: • Flight data (AODB to BRS)


  • AODB-CUPPS:

    Log In: • Log in allowed/denied, • Check-in counter allowable usage duration, • Force log off after duration true/false

    Log Out: • Check check-in desk -in agent log out of , • CUPPS sends check-in desk usage details to IIB, • At force log off time – 5 minutes, a warning will be raised to the user, • At force log off time the user will be automatically logged off, • IIB publishes check-in desk usage details to reporting system


  • AODB-ERP:

    Monthly Billing Run: Eg: • Departure Billable Pax, • Landing Charge, • Parking Charge, • Departing Pax Charge, • CUPPS Charge, • Fuel Standby Charge, • GPU Charge, • PCA Charge, • GSE Charge


  • AODB-FIDS:

    Departure Flight: • Flight schedule (AODB to FIDS), • Resource allocations (RMS to FIDS), •Flight data update (AODB to FIDS), • Gate status updates (FIDS to AODB)

    Arrival Flight: • Flight schedule (AODB to FIDS), • Flight data (AODB to FIDS), • Flight data (FIDS to AODB)


  • AODB-FMS:

    Ad-Hoc & Scheduled Resource Downgrade: • Parking Stand, • Boarding Gate, • Reclaim Carousel


Interface Management


Effective interface management is essential for defining roles, responsibilities, and system boundaries across different stakeholders in projects. Proper interface documentation ensures clear accountability and prevents integration issues during system deployment.


Stakeholder Involvement in Interface Documentation


Interface management is not just limited to IT and software integration—it also plays a critical role in coordinating mechanical, electrical, civil, and structural engineering disciplines.


  • Mechanical Systems:

    • Integration of baggage handling systems (BHS), passenger boarding bridges (PLB), and HVAC controls with automation platforms.

    • Mechanical interface documents define control sequences, sensor feedback loops, and automation triggers


  • Electrical Systems:

    • Coordination of power requirements, UPS integration, and energy metering with building management systems (BMS).

    • Interface agreements ensure proper electrical load distribution, redundancy planning, and fault monitoring


  • Civil:

    • Defining physical installation locations for sensors, cameras, VDGS, and access control systems.

    • Interface agreements clarify mounting specifications, infrastructure support, and compliance with safety regulations
